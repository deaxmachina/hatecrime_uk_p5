////// Concept

Each of the circular shapes corresponds to 100 hate crime incidents reported to the police (London Met Police, London, UK). The definition of “Racist and Religious Hate Crime” in the UK includes non-criminal incidents triggered by the race, ethnicity, or religion of the victim. I chose this topic because I immigrated from Bulgaria around a time of escalating anti-Eastern European sentiments (“benefit seekers”, “stealing our jobs”, “low skilled”, “poor countries”, “go home”...) I know that my own experience unfortunately doesn’t even scratch the surface of the problem. And that shapes on a screen are not enough to make any difference. We see that hate crime is on the rise, but what do we do about it?
The flowing veil surrounding the dark unhappy shapes is my representation of the sometimes invisible ‘shield’ of love, support, friendship. I wanted to find a way to elevate the acts of kindness that happen in the community all the time.
